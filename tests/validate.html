CLI OVERVIEW

qwparse is a structural language analysis CLI. It decomposes words, grades sentences, scans documents for DOG-LATIN, detects null chains, and classifies jurisdiction by typographic form. All output is available as human-readable or machine-readable JSON.

$ qwparse                                     # dashboard
$ qwparse word insurance corporation          # decompose words
$ qwparse sentence "The court hereby..."      # grade sentence
$ qwparse scan document.md                    # full analysis + suggestions
$ qwparse scan --dir ./legal-docs/ --json     # batch evaluate → JSON
$ qwparse dog-latin "THE STATE OF TEXAS"      # DOG-LATIN scan
$ qwparse nullchain "hereby orders..."        # null chain detection
$ qwparse case "JOHN DOE" ":John-Doe:"        # jurisdiction by case
$ echo "text" | qwparse sentence              # stdin support

JURISDICTION MIXING — DOG-LATIN and English on the same document. Per Chicago Manual of Styles Art. 11:147, creates a jurisdictional void.

Contains pronouns — pronouns REMOVE the fact. 'He shall pay' — WHO is 'he'?

Recommendations

Replace adverb-verb chains with noun-fact constructions. A sentence without nouns carries no facts.
Use ONE consistent jurisdiction. Choose parse-syntax (:Colon-Hyphen:) or English, never DOG-LATIN.
Convert all past tense to now-time gerund (-ING) form.
Remove all modal verbs. State facts in now-time: 'FOR THE CLAIMING...' not 'shall claim...'
Replace all pronouns with actual nouns/names.

Key Insight

Sentence structure scores 91/100 — prepositional grounding, gerund verbs, noun-anchored facts. The overall score is penalized because the DOG-LATIN scanner treats all unhyphenated uppercase words as GLOSSA. In correct parse-syntax, uppercase WITH hyphens is valid (:Joel-Thorarinson:), but isolated uppercase tokens (FOR, THE, BY) trigger the scanner. This reveals a calibration opportunity: the scanner should distinguish between parse-syntax uppercase (valid) and DOG-LATIN uppercase (fraudulent).
Pattern confirmed: Court orders score F (37/100) with 10 null chains and 24% DOG-LATIN. Emails score C (68/100) with natural English structure. Correct parse-syntax scores 91/100 on sentence structure. The tools differentiate structural quality as expected.

"IT IS HEREBY ORDERED AND ADJUDGED that the defendant shall forthwith pay"

Issues

Does not begin with a prepositional phrase

Corrections

Begin sentence with a valid preposition: FOR, BY, OF, WITH, IN, AGAINST
Convert verbs to gerund form (-ING) for now-time validity

Remove adverb-verb chains; replace with noun-fact constructions
