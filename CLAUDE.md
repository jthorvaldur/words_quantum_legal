
## Correct Sentence Structure (C.-S.-S.-C.-P.-S.-G.-P.)

Source: :David-Wynn: Miller, continued by :Russell-Jay: Gould.

### The Seven Components

| Component | Definition | Function |
|-----------|------------|----------|
| **CORRECT** | Comprehension of the math interface | The operational grammar is mathematical |
| **SENTENCE** | Joining thoughts between two+ parties | Communication requires at least two |
| **STRUCTURE** | Method of communication | HOW the cargo is arranged in the vessel |
| **COMMUNICATION** | Cargo-word/symbol comprehension | Words are CARGO — they carry meaning |
| **PARSE** | Breaking down words: prefix/root/suffix | Decomposition reveals true meaning |
| **SYNTAX** | Closure of operations — complete thought | A sentence must CLOSE like a circuit |
| **GRAMMAR** | Style of symbol-character communication | The rules governing the symbols |
| **PERFORMANCE** | Doing something positive / conveying facts | The sentence must DO something real |

### Rules for a Correct Sentence

1. **Must begin with a prepositional phrase** using a now-time preposition
   - VALID: FOR, BY, OF, WITH, IN, AGAINST
   - INVALID: TO (future — hasn't happened), FROM (past — doesn't exist)

2. **Nouns are primary fact-carriers**
   - A sentence without nouns carries ZERO facts
   - Nouns anchor meaning; everything else modifies or connects

3. **Verbs must be in gerund form (-ING) for now-time**
   - "claiming" ✓ (now-time, present, living)
   - "claimed" ✗ (past tense = dead time)
   - "shall claim" ✗ (future tense = fiction)

4. **No adverb-verb-adverb-verb chains** (these say NOTHING)
   - "hereby orders that you shall forthwith pay" = NULL CONSTRUCTION
   - No noun anchor → no fact → no contract → no authority

5. **No pronouns** (they REMOVE the fact)
   - "He shall pay" — WHO is "he"? No fact established.
   - Replace with actual noun/name.

6. **Proper closure** — the sentence must complete a thought
   - PREP + ART + ADJ? + NOUN + VERB-ING = minimal complete structure

7. **Hyphens connect, spaces separate**
   - In uppercase (sign language), each unhyphenated word is a SEPARATE SYMBOL
   - Hyphens bond signs into a single quantum of meaning

### The Adverb-Verb Problem: Does the Sentence Say Anything?

The structure `ADVERB + VERB + ADVERB + VERB` is a **null construction**.

```
"The court HEREBY ORDERS that you SHALL FORTHWITH PAY..."

Parse:
  HEREBY  = adverb (modifies nothing concrete)
  ORDERS  = verb (no noun anchor — WHO orders WHAT?)
  SHALL   = modal verb (future tense = fiction — hasn't happened)
  FORTHWITH = adverb (immediately — time command, not fact)
  PAY     = verb (no prepositional closure)

Result: ZERO FACTS CONVEYED. This is a command structure
masquerading as communication. No noun-fact. No closure.
It literally says NOTHING in parse-syntax terms.
```

Compare with correct form:

```
"FOR THE CLAIMING OF THE LAND BY THE LIVING MAN"

Parse:
  FOR     = preposition (now-time, purpose/benefit) ✓
  THE     = article (pointer) ✓
  CLAIMING = gerund/noun (now-time action-as-fact) ✓
  OF      = preposition (belonging/origin) ✓
  THE     = article ✓
  LAND    = noun (FACT-CARRIER: the thing itself) ✓
  BY      = preposition (agency/means) ✓
  THE     = article ✓
  LIVING  = adjective/gerund (now-time descriptor) ✓
  MAN     = noun (FACT-CARRIER: the who) ✓

Result: Two nouns (land, man), prepositional grounding,
now-time verbs, complete closure. This sentence SAYS SOMETHING.
```
