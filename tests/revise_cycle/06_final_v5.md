## IV. ATTORNEY'S FIDUCIARY OBLIGATION AND DUTY OF CANDOR

Under Illinois Rules of Professional Conduct, Rule 1.4, counsel has a duty to keep clients informed of the status and progress of legal matters, including the costs and risks of proposed litigation strategies. Where attorney fees are charged against the marital asset in dispute—here, home equity—counsel has a heightened obligation to communicate whether continued litigation serves the client's financial interest.

The record presents a sequence of events that warrants clarification:

1. Respondent's Financial Affidavit, filed May 16, 2025, listed certain accounts but omitted others;
2. Subsequent discovery revealed the existence of additional accounts;
3. Respondent's counsel has admitted ownership of accounts in correspondence dated May 6, 2026;
4. Those accounts contain $110,041 in documented transfers;
5. Respondent's counsel now characterizes these transfers as "irrelevant."

This sequence does not necessarily indicate improper conduct, but it raises a question: Are positions being adopted based on the underlying facts, or based on what is most defensible in litigation? Counsel should clarify for Respondent whether the irrelevance position reflects a good-faith legal judgment or a litigation tactic.
