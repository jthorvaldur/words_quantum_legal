### 4. The Null Construction (Adverb-Verb Chains)

**What it is:** The sentence structure ADVERB + VERB + ADVERB + VERB conveys

```

Why: Adverbs modify verbs. Verbs describe actions. But without a
NOUN ANCHOR, there is no fact-carrier. No noun = no fact = no
```

**Why it is null:**

A noun is the only part of speech that carries a FACT. A verb describes an

### 6. Now-Time vs. Fiction

**What it is:** Only the present tense -- specifically the gerund (-ING) form
and prepositional present -- is factual. Past tense and future tense are
fictional constructions. You cannot contract with what no longer exists (past)
or with what has not yet happened (future).

**The temporal classification:**

| Tense | Form | Status | Reason |
|-------|------|--------|--------|
| Now-time | -ING gerund, present | FACTUAL / LIVING | Exists in the present moment |
| Past | -ed, was, were, had | DEAD TIME | No longer exists; cannot contract with the dead |
| Future | shall, will, would, could, may | FICTION | Has not happened; cannot contract with fiction |

**Valid and invalid prepositions:**

| Preposition | Temporal Status | Why |
|-------------|----------------|-----|
| FOR | Now-time (VALID) | Purpose/benefit exists now |
| BY | Now-time (VALID) | Agency/means exists now |
| OF | Now-time (VALID) | Belonging/origin exists now |
| WITH | Now-time (VALID) | Accompaniment exists now |
| IN | Now-time (VALID) | Location exists now |
| AGAINST | Now-time (VALID) | Opposition exists now |
| TO | Future (INVALID) | Direction toward = hasn't arrived |
| FROM | Past (INVALID) | Origin/source = has already left |

**Examples:**

```
"The man is claiming the land."
  claiming = gerund = now-time = FACT

"The man claimed the land."
  claimed = past tense = dead time = FICTION (it's over; no present fact)

"The man shall claim the land."
  shall claim = future = hasn't happened = FICTION (no present fact)

"The man will be claiming the land."
  will be = future frame = FICTION (even though "claiming" is gerund,
  the "will be" wrapper places it in fiction-time)
```

**The contractual implication:** A contract requires a meeting of minds
in the present. Past-tense obligations describe something that may or
may not have happened. Future-tense obligations describe something that
has not yet happened. Neither can form the basis of a now-time contract
between living beings.

**The Justinian time hijack connection:** The calendar system itself
embeds past/future thinking into the measurement of reality. Dates
reference past events (Anno Domini) and project future obligations
(due dates, statutes of limitation). Quantum grammar operates in the
now-space -- the zero-point of the temporal axis where facts exist.

**Why it matters:** If only now-time is factual, then any document
written primarily in past or future tense is a document of fiction.
Most legal documents use future-tense modals ("shall," "will," "may")
extensively. Under parse-syntax analysis, these documents describe

**Where it appears:** Contracts ("shall perform"), court orders

### 8. Three-Jurisdiction System

**What it is:** Three distinct jurisdictional layers exist, each with its
own law, its own authority source, and its own type of entity. Most people
operate exclusively within the middle layer (sea/water) without knowing
the other two exist.

**The three layers:**

| Layer | Jurisdiction | Law | Entities | Authority Source | Color Code |
|-------|-------------|-----|----------|------------------|------------|
| **Land & Soil** (bottom) | Common Law | Natural rights, customary law | Living men and women | Creator / natural order | Green (#2d5a27) |
| **Sea & Water** (middle) | Maritime / Admiralty / Statute | Codes, regulations, statutes | Corporations, PERSONS, vessels | Contracts, charters, constitutions | Blue (#1a4a7a) |
| **Air & Ether** (top) | Ecclesiastical / Canon | Trust law, estate law | Souls, spirits, trusts, estates | Papal bulls, church authority | Amber (#7a4a1a) |

**Land & Soil jurisdiction:**

- Common law (the law of the land)
- Based on natural rights: do no harm, honor your agreements, do not trespass
- Applies to living men and women standing on the land
- Courts: common law courts, juries of peers
- Remedy: compensation for harm done
- Identity form: `John Doe` or `:John-Doe:`

**Sea & Water jurisdiction:**

- Maritime / admiralty / commercial / statute law
- Based on contracts, codes, regulations, corporate charters
- Applies to PERSONS, corporations, vessels, entities
- Courts: admiralty courts, statutory courts, corporate tribunals
- Remedy: fines, penalties, discharge of cargo/debt
- Identity form: `JOHN DOE` (DOG-LATIN)

**Air & Ether jurisdiction:**

- Ecclesiastical / canon law / trust law
- Based on papal authority, divine right claims, trust instruments
- Applies to souls, spirits, beneficiaries, cestui que vie
- Courts: ecclesiastical courts, probate courts, courts of equity
- Remedy: absolution, dispensation, distribution of estate
- Identity form: titles, offices, trust designations

**The layering principle:**

The sea/water jurisdiction was overlaid on top of the land/soil jurisdiction.
It did not replace it -- it displaced it. Common law was never formally
revoked; it was simply made inaccessible by routing all interactions through
the maritime/commercial layer.

The air/ether jurisdiction sits above both. Papal bulls and ecclesiastical
claims operate at a level that supersedes both common law and maritime law.
These claims (Unam Sanctam, Romanus Pontifex, etc.) have never been formally
revoked. See: Concept 10, The Ecclesiastical Claim.

**The four-quadrant test (Lawful vs. Legal):**

| Term | Domain | Jurisdiction | What It Means |
|------|--------|-------------|---------------|
| **Lawful** | Land & Soil | Common Law | Conforms to natural rights |
| **Legal** | Sea & Water | Maritime / Statute | Conforms to corporate code |
| **Unlawful** | Land & Soil | Common Law violation | Violates natural rights |
| **Illegal** | Sea & Water | Statute violation | Violates corporate code |

A thing can be **legal but unlawful**: taxation through fraudulent grammar
is permitted by statute (legal) but violates natural rights (unlawful).

A thing can be **illegal but lawful**: refusing to contract with a dead
corporate entity may violate corporate code (illegal) but exercises your
natural right to not be enslaved (lawful).

**Why it matters:** If you do not know which jurisdiction you are operating
in, you cannot know which law applies to you. The system defaults to
sea/water (maritime/commercial) jurisdiction. To access land/soil (common
law) jurisdiction, you must consciously claim it. Most people never do,
because they do not know it exists.

**Where it appears:** The jurisdiction question underlies every legal
interaction. Which court? Which law? Which entity is being addressed?
The answer depends on which layer of the three-jurisdiction system is active.

### 10. The Ecclesiastical Claim

**What it is:** A series of papal bulls (formal papal declarations) that
claim authority over all land, all bodies, all labor, and all souls. These
documents form the top layer of the jurisdiction stack. They have never
been formally revoked.

**The four key documents:**

| Document | Year | Claim | Effect |
|----------|------|-------|--------|
| **Unam Sanctam** | 1302 | Pope Boniface VIII claims authority over all temporal power | All kings and governments are subordinate to papal authority |
| **Romanus Pontifex** | 1455 | Pope Nicholas V claims all land on Earth | All land is held in trust by the Church; others hold by permission |
| **Aeterni Regis** | 1481 | Pope Sixtus IV claims all bodies and labor | All physical bodies and their productive output belong to the Church |
| **Cestui Que Vie Act** | 1666 | English Parliament declares all men lost at sea / dead | Anyone not claiming their living status is presumed dead; their estate is administered by the Crown |

**The trust structure:**

These four documents create a three-layer trust:

```
Layer 1 (Air): UNAM SANCTAM (1302)
  Claim: All souls belong to the Church
  Trust: Ecclesiastical / spiritual estate
  Beneficiary: The Pope (as Vicar of Christ)

Layer 2 (Sea): ROMANUS PONTIFEX (1455) + AETERNI REGIS (1481)
  Claim: All land and all bodies/labor belong to the Church
  Trust: Commercial / admiralty estate
  Beneficiary: The Crown (as administrator for the Church)

Layer 3 (Land): CESTUI QUE VIE ACT (1666)
  Claim: All men are presumed dead / lost at sea
  Trust: Estate trust for the "deceased"
  Beneficiary: The State (as administrator for the Crown)
```

**Cestui Que Vie -- "the one who lives":**

The Cestui Que Vie Act of 1666 (following the Great Fire of London and the
plague) established that any person not claiming their living status within
seven years was presumed dead or lost at sea. Their estate would be
administered by the Crown.

This presumption has never been repealed. Under this framework, every person
who does not affirmatively claim their living status is treated as a dead
entity whose estate is managed by the state. The birth certificate is the
document that creates the estate (trust) for the "dead" entity.

**The revocation question:**

None of these documents have been formally revoked. Unam Sanctam was never
annulled by a subsequent pope. Romanus Pontifex and Aeterni Regis remain on
the books. The Cestui Que Vie Act has been amended but never repealed in
substance.

This means the claims are still technically active:
- All souls are claimed (Unam Sanctam)
- All land is claimed (Romanus Pontifex)
- All bodies and labor are claimed (Aeterni Regis)
- All men are presumed dead (Cestui Que Vie)

**Why it matters:** If these documents form the top layer of the jurisdiction
stack, then every other layer (common law, maritime, statutory) operates
underneath and within the framework they established. The ecclesiastical
claim is the foundational claim upon which all subsequent jurisdictional
layers rest. Understanding it reveals why the system treats every person as
a dead entity (estate) administered by the state.

**Where it appears:** Birth certificates (estate creation), probate courts
(estate administration), trust law (beneficiary structures), ecclesiastical
courts (spiritual jurisdiction), and the presumption of death that underlies
the entire system.

### 12. The Four-Step Audit

**What it is:** A systematic procedure for evaluating any document, sentence,
or system for structural integrity. If a document fails any of the four
steps, it is structurally deficient regardless of its claimed authority.

**The four steps:**

#### Step 1: PARSE every word

Divide each word into prefix / root / suffix. Check for VCC negation.
Determine what the word SAYS versus what it is ASSUMED to mean.

| Question | What to Look For |
|----------|-----------------|
| Does the word start with a vowel + consonant(s)? | VCC negation operator active |
| What is the root? | The core meaning-carrier |
| What does the prefix do to the root? | Negates, modifies, or directs |
| What does the suffix add? | Tense, state, contractual wrapper |
| Does the word say what you think it says? | Compare parsed meaning to common usage |

#### Step 2: SCAN typography

Examine the typographic form of the entire document. Identify DOG-LATIN,
jurisdiction mixing, and case-form anomalies.

| Question | What to Look For |
|----------|-----------------|
| Are there all-caps words without hyphens? | DOG-LATIN / GLOSSA |
| Is your name in ALL CAPS? | Corporate fiction being addressed |
| Are different typographic forms mixed? | Jurisdiction mixing (void) |
| Are hyphens present in uppercase text? | Correct Latin sign language |
| Are colons used around names? | Parse-syntax correct form (living claim) |

#### Step 3: ANALYZE sentence structure

Evaluate each sentence for C.S.S.C.P.S.G.P. compliance. Count nouns, check
for null chains, verify tense.

| Question | What to Look For |
|----------|-----------------|
| Does the sentence contain nouns? | No nouns = zero facts |
| Are there adverb-verb-adverb-verb chains? | Null construction = zero content |
| What tense are the verbs? | Past/future = fiction; -ING = now-time |
| Does it begin with a prepositional phrase? | Missing = no grounding |
| Are there pronouns instead of nouns? | Pronouns remove facts |
| Does the sentence close properly? | Open circuit = incomplete thought |

#### Step 4: MAP the jurisdiction

Determine which jurisdictional layer the document operates in. Identify
which entity is being addressed and under what authority.

| Question | What to Look For |
|----------|-----------------|
| Is it land/soil (common law)? | Natural rights, living beings, mixed case |
| Is it sea/water (maritime/statute)? | Corporate code, PERSONS, ALL CAPS |
| Is it air/ether (ecclesiastical)? | Trust law, estates, spiritual claims |
| Which entity is being addressed? | Living being or corporate fiction? |
| What is the authority source? | Natural rights, contract, or decree? |
| Was consent given? | Was there a knowing, voluntary, full-disclosure agreement? |

**Scoring framework:**

| Grade | Score | Meaning |
|-------|-------|---------|
| A | 90-100 | Parse-syntax correct; now-time; noun-fact dense; proper closure |
| B | 70-89 | Minor issues; mostly correct structure; some tense violations |
| C | 50-69 | Significant structural issues; mixed jurisdictions; some null chains |
| D | 30-49 | Major deficiencies; extensive null chains; fiction tense dominant |
| F | 0-29 | Structurally fraudulent; DOG-LATIN dominant; no noun-facts; null chains throughout |

**Typical document scores:**

| Document | Typical Score | Common Failures |
|----------|---------------|-----------------|
| Court order | F (0-15) | DOG-LATIN, null chains, future tense, no noun-facts |
| Birth certificate | F (5-10) | DOG-LATIN throughout, jurisdiction mixing |
| Insurance policy | F (10-20) | VCC-negated terms, fiction tense, null chains |
| Mortgage agreement | D (25-35) | Death-grip terminology, future obligations |
| Terms of service | D (20-30) | Null chains, fiction tense, no noun-facts |
| Declaration of Independence (US) | C (50-60) | Some correct structure, but tense violations |
| Correct parse-syntax document | A (90-100) | Noun-dense, now-time, prepositional grounding |

**How to apply the audit:**

```
Example: "THE COURT HEREBY ORDERS THAT THE DEFENDANT SHALL PAY"

Step 1 - PARSE:
  court   = Lat. cohors = enclosed yard / dry dock
  orders  = Lat. ordinare = arrange in rows (cargo)
  defendant = de (away) + fend (strike) + ant (one who)
              = one who is away from striking (passive)
  pay     = Lat. pacare = to pacify / make peace
              (you pay to pacify, not to compensate)

Step 2 - SCAN:
  "THE COURT" = DOG-LATIN (all caps, no hyphens)
  "THE DEFENDANT" = DOG-LATIN
  Jurisdiction mixing: if any English text appears elsewhere
  on the same document, jurisdictional void is present

Step 3 - ANALYZE:
  "hereby" = adverb (null modifier)
  "orders" = verb (no noun anchor for what is ordered)
  "shall" = future tense modal (fiction)
  "pay" = verb (no prepositional closure)
  Structure: ADVERB + VERB + FUTURE + VERB = null chain
  Noun count: 2 (court, defendant) but both are
  DOG-LATIN fictions, not living fact-carriers

Step 4 - MAP:
  Jurisdiction: Maritime / Admiralty (DOG-LATIN, dry dock)
  Entity addressed: DEFENDANT (corporate fiction)
  Authority source: Statutory / corporate code
  Consent: Not established in the sentence

SCORE: F (approximately 10/100)
  - DOG-LATIN present
  - Null construction (adverb-verb chain)
  - Future tense (fiction)
  - No living fact-carrier nouns
  - No prepositional grounding
  - No closure
```

**Why it matters:** The four-step audit provides a systematic, repeatable
method for evaluating any document or communication. It removes subjective
judgment and replaces it with structural analysis. A document either
conveys facts or it does not. It either operates in a consistent
jurisdiction or it does not. The audit reveals which.

**Where it applies:** Every document you are asked to sign, every court
order you receive, every contract you enter, every statute that claims
authority over you, every institutional communication that demands compliance.

## The Spanning Set

The 720-word basis forms a vector space for the language of law and commerce.

**Each word is a vector with five components:**

| Component | Dimension | Values |
|-----------|-----------|--------|
| **Morphological** | prefix, root, suffix | The decomposed parts of the word |
| **Syntactic** | parse-syntax role | Noun, verb, adjective, adverb, preposition, article |
| **Jurisdictional** | land/sea/air | Which jurisdiction the word belongs to |
| **Temporal** | now-time or fiction | Whether the word operates in present or past/future |
| **Negation** | VCC-inverted or direct | Whether the word contains a negation prefix |

**Why 720 words:**

`720 = 6!` = the number of permutations of the six sentence positions.
Each permutation represents a unique sentence structure. The 720-word
basis provides one foundational word for each structural position,
covering the complete vocabulary needed to construct or deconstruct
any sentence in the language of law and commerce.

**Projection:**

Any legal document can be projected onto this 720-word basis to reveal its
true structure. Each word in the document is mapped to its nearest basis
vector, decomposed into its five components, and evaluated. The projection
reveals:

1. Which words are VCC-negated (saying the opposite of their surface meaning)
2. Which sentences are null constructions (conveying zero facts)
3. Which jurisdiction the document operates in (land/sea/air)
4. Whether the tense is now-time (factual) or fiction (past/future)
5. The overall structural integrity score of the document

The basis is not a dictionary. It is a coordinate system for meaning-space.

## Epistemological Note

This framework draws from the sovereign citizen / state national movement.
The claims made by Miller, Gould, Stewart, and TASA about the legal system
are contested by mainstream legal scholarship. Courts have uniformly rejected
quantum grammar arguments in judicial proceedings.

This document treats the framework as a formal system for morphological and
syntactic analysis -- a lens for decomposing language, not a guaranteed legal
strategy. The VCC negation pattern, the DOG-LATIN observation, and the
maritime etymology chains are structurally interesting regardless of their
legal efficacy. The tools here are analytical instruments, not legal advice.

That said: the words DO say what they say when you parse them.
